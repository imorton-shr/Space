The Sun is a giant ball of hot, glowing gas! It gives us light and keeps us warm. The Sun is SO big that one million Earths could fit inside it! 🌟

⭐ Fun Fact: The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old!

The Moon is Earth's best friend in space! It goes around our planet and changes shape in the sky. Sometimes it's round like a ball, and sometimes it looks like a banana! 🍌

🌕 Fun Fact: Astronauts have walked on the Moon!

The Solar System is our home in space! It consists of the Sun at the center with 8 planets, moons, and asteroids all orbiting around it. Everything in our Solar System is held together by the Sun's powerful gravity! ☀️

🌍 Fun Fact: It takes Earth 365 days (one year) to orbit completely around the Sun!

There are 8 planets in our solar system! They all go around the Sun. Can you learn their names?

Stars are like tiny sparkles in the night sky! They are actually giant balls of fire, just like our Sun. There are more stars in space than grains of sand on all the beaches! 🏖️

✨ Fun Fact: The closest star to Earth (after the Sun) is called Proxima Centauri!

Our Solar System lives inside a huge galaxy called the Milky Way! A galaxy is like a giant family of billions and billions of stars held together by gravity. The Milky Way is so big that light takes 100,000 years to travel from one side to the other! 🌌

✨ Fun Fact: The Milky Way has over 200 BILLION stars in it!

An eclipse happens when the Moon or Earth block sunlight! During a solar eclipse, the Moon moves in front of the Sun and creates a shadow on Earth. During a lunar eclipse, Earth moves between the Sun and Moon, and Earth's shadow falls on the Moon, making it look reddish! 🌙

☀️ Solar Eclipse

The Moon passes in front of the Sun, blocking its light and creating a shadow on Earth!

🌙 Lunar Eclipse

Earth passes between the Sun and Moon, casting Earth's shadow on the Moon and making it look red!

🔭 Fun Fact: You should never look directly at the Sun during a solar eclipse without special eclipse glasses!
